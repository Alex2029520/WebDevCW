Protect&Preserve OCEANS

Rivers and lakes are crucial to our survival, covering a significant portion of the Earths surface and serving as vital sources of freshwater, food, and energy.

Human activities have been damaging marine ecosystems for years. Now is the time to take action and make a change.

To preserve and sustain ocean health, it is imperative to stop pollution, end overfishing, and initiate projects to conserve of marine biodiversity globally.

Objectives for Sustainable Development

These goals set the foundation for preserving and protecting our oceans for the future.

REDUCE POLLUTION

Enforce stricter waste disposal laws to stop pollutants from contaminating marine ecosystems.

PROTECT AND RESTORE ECOSYSTEMS

Launch large-scale coral reef restoration projects to rejuvenate damaged reefs.

REDUCE OCEAN ACIDIFICATION

Encourage the use of renewable energy to reduce carbon emissions.

FISHING Sustainably

Implement catch limits informed by scientific assessments to ensure fish populations are harvested at sustainable levels.

Protect Coastal and Marine Ecosystems

Create marine protected areas which have areas that are restricted

SUSTAINABLE USE OF MARINE RESOURCES

Encourage the development and use of marine biotechnology that allows for the sustainable extraction of marine resources.

Enhance Ocean Health Research and Technology

Fund and promote health development programmes.

INTERNATIONAL SEA LAW

Enforce international sea laws to ensure sustainable fishing, protect marine biodiversity, and prevent illegal activities.”
