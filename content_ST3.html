Exploring Marine Ecosystems

Marine ecosystems are aquatic environments with high levels of dissolved salt. These include the open ocean, the deep-sea ocean, and coastal marine ecosystems, each with unique physical and biological characteristics. Marine ecosystems provide a vital source of protein for millions of people worldwide. Fish, shellfish, and other marine organisms are essential for human nutrition and livelihoods.

Threats to Ocean Health

Plastic is not the only pollutant that endangers the balance and health of marine ecosystems. Many pollutants originate from human activities and pose a serious threat to the ocean.

Protection of Life Below Water

SDG 14 is essential for human well-being, economic prosperity, and the health of our planet by safeguarding underwater life. Oceans and seas cover 70 percent of our planet, playing a vital role in our existence. We rely on these aquatic ecosystems for food, energy, and water. Unfortunately, human activities have caused significant damage to these precious resources.

How Individuals Can Make a Difference ?

Every donation, no matter the size, can make a significant impact! Instead of buying bottled water, consider boiling, filtering, chlorinating, or using rainwater. Reduce waste, as much of what we discard on land eventually reaches the oceans.

Importance of Coral Reefs

Coral reefs are essential marine ecosystems that sustain a wide variety of marine life. They offer food, shelter, and breeding grounds for numerous species. However, coral reefs are under threat from climate change and ocean acidification.

Marine Biodiversity

Marine biodiversity encompasses the diverse life forms in the ocean, such as fish, corals, and marine mammals. Protecting this biodiversity is vital for maintaining ecosystem balance and supporting human well-being.

Sustainable Fishing Practices

Implementing sustainable fishing practices, like preventing overfishing and safeguarding marine habitats, is crucial for sustaining healthy fish populations and conserving marine ecosystems for future generations.
