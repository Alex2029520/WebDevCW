Exploring Marine Ecosystems

Marine ecosystems are aquatic environments with high levels of dissolved salt. These include the open ocean, the deep-sea ocean, and coastal marine ecosystems, each with different physical and biological characteristics.Marine ecosystems provide a vital source of protein for millions of people worldwide. Fish, shellfish, and other marine organisms are essential for human nutrition and livelihoods.

Threats to Ocean Health

Plastic is not the only type of pollution that threatens the balance and health of marine ecosystems. Most pollutants come from human activity and pose a serious threat to the ocean.

Protection of Life Below Water

SDG 14 is essential for human well-being, economic prosperity, and the health of our planet by safeguarding underwater life. Oceans and seas cover 70 percent of our planet, playing a vital role in our existence. We rely on these aquatic ecosystems for food, energy, and water. Unfortunately, human activities have caused significant damage to these precious resources.

How Individuals Can Make a Difference ?

Any donation, big or small, can make a difference! Avoid buying bottled water - opt for boiling, filtering, chlorinating, or using rainwater. Reduce waste - much of the waste we produce on land ends up in the oceans.

Importance of Coral Reefs

Coral reefs are vital marine ecosystems that support a diverse range of marine life. They provide food, protection, and breeding grounds for many species. However, coral reefs are facing threats such as climate change and ocean acidification.

Marine Biodiversity

Marine biodiversity refers to the variety of life forms in the ocean, including fish, corals, marine mammals, and more. Preserving marine biodiversity is crucial for ecosystem balance and human well-being.

Sustainable Fishing Practices

Practicing sustainable fishing methods, such as avoiding overfishing and protecting marine habitats, is essential for maintaining healthy fish populations and preserving marine ecosystems for future generations.
