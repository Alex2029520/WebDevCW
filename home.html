CONSERVE & SUSTAIN OCEANS

Oceans and seas are crucial to our survival, covering 70% of the Earth's surface and serving as vital sources of food, energy, and water.

All marine ecosystems are harmfully affected by human activities throughout the years. It is the time to take action. Time for change.

To preserve and sustain ocean health, it is imperative to stop pollution, end overfishing, and initiate projects to conserve of marine biodiversity globally.

TARGETS FOR THE SUSTAINABLE GOAL

These objectives define the ultimate goals we must achieve to sustain ocean conservation effectively.

REDUCE MARINE POLLUTION

Implement stricter regulations on waste disposal to prevent pollutants from entering marine environments.

PROTECT AND RESTORE ECOSYSTEMS

Launch large-scale coral reef restoration projects to rejuvenate damaged reefs.

REDUCE OCEAN ACIDIFICATION

Promote renewable energy sources to decrease carbon emissions.

SUSTAINABLE FISHING

Enforce catch limits based on scientific assessments to ensure fish populations are harvested at sustainable rates.

CONSERVE COASTAL AND MARINE AREAS

Establish marine protected areas where fishing and drilling are prohibited.

END SUBSIDIES CONTRIBUTING TO OVERFISHING

Reform financial policies to eliminate subsidies for fishing practices that contribute to overexploitation.

SUSTAINABLE USE OF MARINE RESOURCES

Encourage the development and use of marine biotechnology that allows for the sustainable extraction of marine resources.

INCREASE RESEARCH TECHNOLOGY FOR OCEAN HEALTH

Fund and promote the development of advanced oceanographic research technology.

SUPPORT SMALL SCALE FISHERS

Provide training and resources to small-scale fishers for adopting sustainable fishing practices.

ENFORCE INTERNATIONAL SEA LAW

Strengthen international cooperation to ensure the protection of marine environments.
